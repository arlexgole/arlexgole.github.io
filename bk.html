Background

January 2020 - Ongoing

Team: Allison Mui, Arlex Gole

One of the biggest challenges for nonprofits is raising funds effectively. From my experience working with smaller non-profits, a lot of time is spent planning and executing fundraisers.

The people working for non-profits are extremely passionate about helping solve important problems such as poverty, human trafficking, and climate change. Many are also highly experienced in addressing challenges in their unique domains. However, rather than focusing on the solutions, they often have to spend time organizing and managing the logistics of a fundraiser.

Bloo Kiwi is a project that explores the power of fashion to spread awareness about important issues and help nonprofits raise funds. It is an attempt to reallocate some of the capital from the trillion-dollar fashion industry to the nonprofit sector.

Website

I customized the Shopify theme using HTML, CSS, and Liquid (Shopify's templating language).

Each collection is dedicated to a unique nonprofit and 50% of the profits from the collection will be donated to the respective nonprofit.

Each nonprofit also has a dedicated page that provides information on the nonprofit's impact and problem space.

Blog shares the stories of frontline heroes at various nonprofits.

Ensuring that the custom pages are responsive across different devices.

Takeaways

The project is still in the early stages and there is a lot more to do, but I have already learned a lot. One of the biggest challenges was working with a base template. The templates on Shopify provide a solid foundation and allow you to easily customize certain things like font and color using a GUI. However, bigger changes to the template like adding a fixed side navigation or creating an entirely custom page required big changes to the code.

It was an eye-opening experience to be both the designer advocating for a better user experience and the developer managing the technical constraints. As a result, I gained a deeper understanding and appreciation for balancing design and technical constraints. I learned that certain design changes can seem very minimal as a designer but can require tremendous development effort due to the way a framework is setup. On the other hand, the front-end code could be set up in a flexible way that synergies well with certain design changes.
